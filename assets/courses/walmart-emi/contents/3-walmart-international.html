Walmart International & Sam's Club

Walmart International

  • Walmart became an international company in 1991.
  • Walmart's international operations comprises of 6,265 stores and 800,000 workers in 26 countries outside the United States
  • Walmart International is currently the fastest growing part of the business.

Sam's Club

  • Sam's Club is a chain of warehouse clubs that sell groceries and general merchandise, often in bulk. The first Sam's Club opened in 1983. Currently there are more than 600 clubs in the U.S. and 100 clubs internationally. There are three kinds of memberships of Sam's Club: Sam's Plus, Sam's Business and Sam's Savings. Each of these memberships provides unique benefits and convenience to the customers. However, non-members can make purchases either by buying a one-day membership or paying a surcharge based on the price of the purchase.
  • Sam's Club employs about 110,000 associates in the U.S. and the average club size is 134,000 square feet.
  • Most clubs also have specialty services, such as a pharmacy, an optical department, a photo center or a tire and battery center
